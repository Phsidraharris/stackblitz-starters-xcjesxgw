Evening — Setting the Stage
It began with a question:
What does justice mean to you?
The sprint opened with an icebreaker — say your name, share your pronouns, and do one thing while claiming you're doing the opposite. Laughter filled TinkerSpace. Then came the harder question. Participants answered with “fairness,” “freedom,” “equality,” and — memorably — “the opposite of injustice.”

Our Reflections
What we'd do differently
Honest reflections from the organising team.
Area
What Went Well
What Could Improve
Icebreakers & Activities
Fun, well-timed breaks that energised the room
More body-based activities; icebreaker in regional language; stronger connection to hackathon theme
Problem Statements
Thematic diversity; real justicemaking needs; strong data-as-facts approach
Less wordy language; clearer expectations on technology scope
Reflections & Sharing
Safe space created; participants gave rich, justice-connected reflections
Better Malayalam/English translation; deeper connection with technology angle
Solution & Prototyping
Freedom in format and content; creative autonomy
More time for prototyping; more technical guidance; case studies were too complex
Mentorship
Equal distribution; mentors given choice of problem statements
Structured feedback on capabilities; better utilisation of pre-prepared resource list
